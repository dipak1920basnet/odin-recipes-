Turkey Goulash

Turkey goulash is a delightful twist on the traditional Hungarian beef goulash, offering a leaner and lighter alternative with the same bold flavors. This dish showcases tender chunks of turkey meat simmered in a rich and aromatic paprika-infused sauce. The combination of sweet and, optionally, smoky paprika lends a depth of flavor that's both comforting and inviting. As the turkey cooks, it soaks up the savory sauce, becoming succulent and incredibly flavorful. The addition of bell peppers and diced tomatoes adds color and freshness to the dish, while a touch of chicken broth provides the perfect balance of moisture and richness. One of the hallmarks of turkey goulash is its versatility; you can serve it over a bed of egg noodles, rice, or mashed potatoes, making it a satisfying meal for any occasion. As the goulash simmers on the stove, the tantalizing aroma fills the kitchen, offering a preview of the mouthwatering experience to come. When it's time to serve, a dollop of sour cream and a sprinkle of fresh parsley on top provide a creamy contrast to the robust sauce, elevating the dish to a culinary masterpiece. Whether you're preparing it for a family dinner or a special gathering, turkey goulash is sure to become a beloved addition to your repertoire of comforting, flavorful dishes.

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 pounds of turkey breast or thigh meat, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons sweet paprika
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional, for extra flavor)
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup sour cream (for serving)
  • Chopped fresh parsley (for garnish)
  • Cooked egg noodles, rice, or mashed potatoes (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. In a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent, about 3-5 minutes.
  3. Add the minced garlic and sauté for another 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add the turkey pieces and cook until they are lightly browned on all sides.
  5. Sprinkle the sweet paprika and, if using, the smoked paprika over the turkey and stir to coat evenly.
  6. Add the chopped bell pepper and cook for a few minutes until it starts to soften.
  7. Pour in the diced tomatoes (with their juices) and chicken broth. Stir well.
  8. Add the bay leaf and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the goulash simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the turkey is tender and the sauce has thickened.
  10. While the goulash is simmering, prepare your choice of side dish - cooked egg noodles, rice, or mashed potatoes.
  11. Once the goulash is ready, remove the bay leaf and discard it.
  12. Serve the turkey goulash hot over your chosen side dish.
  13. Garnish each serving with a dollop of sour cream and a sprinkle of chopped fresh parsley.
